## 1. Interviewee Role & Background
- Role: OMT Application Adjudicator
- Experience: 3-4 years of reviewing applications
- Background: Geologist at University of Free State, originally from Canada
- Discipline: Earth Sciences, with broad interdisciplinary exposure

## 2. Current Review Process Description
- Receives applications via OMT website link
- Parcels applications by level (Master's, PhD, Sabbatical)
- Reviews 5-10 applications per sitting to avoid "adjudication fatigue"
- Uses a rubric with 1-4 point scale for specific categories
- Final 5-point overall assessment
- Allocates careful time to value both the project and the proposer

## 3. Pain Points & Challenges
- Rigid rubric categories that don't accommodate diverse backgrounds
- Limited scoring range (1-4, then 5-point final scale)
- Difficulty comparing grades across different institutions
- Challenges evaluating edge cases and non-traditional candidates
- Risk of unconscious bias
- Time pressure of pro-bono work

## 4. What They Value in Applications
- Passion over pure cleverness
- Positive outlook for change
- Aspiration and leadership potential
- Consideration of candidate's circumstances
- Project feasibility
- Budget appropriateness
- Potential for local/global impact
- Character and tenacity

## 5. Views on AI/Technology
- Positive attitude toward AI assistance
- Sees potential for:
  - Logic validation
  - Stakeholder perception assessment
  - Data scraping
  - Subject matter expertise support
- Concerns about:
  - Uneven AI usage among adjudicators
  - Potential wrong answers
  - Connection reliability

## 6. Suggestions & Ideas
- More nebulous, flexible definition of excellence
- Broader understanding of candidate potential
- More sensitivity to unique circumstances
- Ability to provide nuanced comments
- Better mechanism for explaining edge cases

## 7. Key Direct Quotes
1. "I always value passion higher than I value cleverness. You can always teach passion but you can't build passion always."

2. "Excellence, true excellence can be measured whether it is from a lay person's perspective or it can be from an absolute disciplined leader."

3. "A definition of excellence that was a little bit more sensitive... could shift them right into a realm where they could be funded versus where they would not be."

4. "I do my best to adjudicate from multiple different angles because excellence... can be measured from different perspectives."

5. "Not every applicant has availability of word processing, spell-check engines."
