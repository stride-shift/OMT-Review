## 1. Interviewee Role & Background
- Role: OMT Application Reviewer (Creative Arts, Primarily Fine Arts)
- Years of Experience: Approximately 3 years
- Discipline: Fine Arts, sits on Department of Higher Education and Training (DHET) national panel for creative outputs review
- Current Position: Fine Arts Department at Rhodes University

## 2. Current Review Process Description
- Comparative review approach
- Initial overview of all applications 
- Skims through materials to get holistic perspective
- Uses a 1-4 scoring rubric
- Detailed review of each component (portfolio, academic records, motivation)
- "Retrofit" scoring - adjusting final rating based on overall impression
- Looks for coherence across application components

## 3. Pain Points & Challenges
- Interpreting academic achievements out of context
- Difficulty assessing "significance to society" questions
- Challenges with "personal motivation" section
- Unreliable references
- Misalignment between written proposals and portfolio quality
- Potential AI-generated application content

## 4. What They Value in Applications
- Portfolio as critical assessment element
- Technical and conceptual sophistication
- Coherence between proposed study and current work
- Contextual annotation of portfolio
- Clear articulation of research vision
- Evidence of practical excellence

## 5. Views on AI/Technology
- Somewhat skeptical of AI
- Sees potential for:
  - Initial document filtering
  - Generating administrative summaries
  - Flagging inconsistencies
- Strongly believes creative assessment requires human interpretation
- Concerned about instrumentalist application responses

## 6. Suggestions & Ideas
- Require at least one reference from research supervisor
- More space/guidance for articulating personal values
- Potential for AI-generated program information summaries
- Initial screening for document completeness
- Clearer rubric design

## 7. Key Direct Quotes
1. "Assessing art requires time and a human element of interpretation, which cannot be easily short-circuited."
2. "The portfolio is critical... one can talk things up, and especially with AI, it's very easy to write a convincing proposal."
3. "I tend to be more strict if somebody's going to be doing an overseas-based study and it's going to be costing OMT a lot of money."
4. "Practice in its nature is exploratory... it's very difficult to know before you've even set out on this journey where you're going to end up."
5. "There needs to be a kind of coherence where the way the person articulates their work aligns with the work."

## 8. Unique Insights
- Deep understanding of creative practice assessment
- Nuanced view of AI's potential role
- Emphasis on interpretative, holistic review process
- Personal experience as an OMT scholarship recipient
